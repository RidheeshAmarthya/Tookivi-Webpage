Interpersonal Communication

In simple terms, communication between two individuals is referred to as Interpersonal communication. It is considered to be a very basic means of communication. These communication skills can be improved by practice, feedback, and knowledge of the subject at hand. Interpersonal communication can take place when two people are at the same place and are aware of each other’s presence. It can be unintentional or impromptu. It can range from simple gestures, such as hand waving, expressions, body language or clothing. The main point that is important is that there should be a clear cut message that has to be sent by the sender, and it should be received by the receiver.

Exchange of Ideas is Vital to Communication

Communicate

Interpersonal Communication came into existence when people started to exchange ideas and thoughts using well constructed thoughts. This type of communication is a type of communication through which people can communicate their feelings, ideas, emotions and information with one another. It can be in verbal or non verbal form. Interpersonal communication is not just limited to just what is being said and what is being received. It equally comprises how it is said, what body language is used, and the emotions carried when conveying the message.
